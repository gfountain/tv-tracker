✨ What's New

The most recent additions and changes to Marquee. See Release History at the bottom for the full changelog.

  • Daily email digest — emails are now sent once per day at 6 AM Eastern instead of immediately. The digest includes a smart summary, a full Airing Today section with network info and Plex flags, and a Show Updates section for anything that changed. Quiet days still get an email — "All quiet today."
  • Timezone setting — a new Timezone option in ⚙️ Settings lets you pick your US timezone (Eastern, Central, Mountain, Pacific). All dates throughout the app and in your digest email use your selected timezone. Saved to Supabase so it syncs across all your devices.
  • Watching tab filter views — five filter chips on the Watching tab: All, Unwatched, Upcoming, Filming, Ended. Each shows a count badge. Unwatched only shows shows with genuinely available aired episodes — not future-dated ones.
  • New alert types — alerts now fire for: air date rescheduled, show entered production (now filming), and any episode airing today (not just season premieres). The in-app detection and hourly background job both cover all alert types.
  • Season premiere indicator — list rows now show "🎬 S3 premieres Tomorrow" for season premieres instead of the generic episode format.
  • Hide watched episodes — watched episodes hidden by default within each season. "👁 Show Watched (N)" in the season header reveals them.
  • Episode summaries — tap any episode title with a ▾ chevron to expand the TMDB plot summary inline.
  • Streaming icons in list view — provider logos appear in each list row to the left of episode progress.
  • Plex toggle — "📦 Add to Plex" in the detail panel flags shows in your Plex library. Shows a gold P badge in list rows and appears in digest emails.
  • Improved recommendations — "You Might Also Like" now merges TMDB /recommendations and /similar, with recommendations ranked first for better franchise connections.
  • Mobile drawer — ☰ hamburger on mobile opens a slide-out drawer with all navigation. Swipe right from left edge to open.
  • Horizontal season pill selector — replaces vertical accordion stack. Auto-selects first unwatched season. Pills show ✓ (done), · (in progress), or plain (not started).
  • Ratings panel — clicking ⭐ in any detail panel opens a slide-out with RT, Metacritic, IMDb, and TMDB scores.

🚀 Getting Started

Signing In

On the sign-in screen you have three options:

  • Email + Password — enter your email and password and click Sign In.
  • Sign Up — switch to Sign Up, enter email and password. Check your inbox if email confirmation is required.
  • Magic Link — enter your email and click "✨ Send Magic Link Instead." A link arrives by email that signs you in instantly, no password needed.

Session Persistence

Marquee stays signed in across browser restarts. You'll only be signed out if you explicitly click 🚪 Sign Out or your session expires.

Privacy

Every piece of data is scoped to your account via Supabase Row Level Security. Even users sharing the same Marquee instance cannot see each other's data. This is enforced at the database level, not just in the UI.

Inline List Expansion

Click anywhere on a show row (except the poster or title) to expand it inline for full season/episode tracking. The ▾ chevron shows expand state. Click the poster or title to open the full slide-out detail panel.

Auto-Complete

When you mark the last aired episode of an Ended or Cancelled show, Marquee automatically moves it to Completed, logs it in the Feed, and shows a 🎉 toast. Only fires for definitively finished shows — not Returning or In Production.

🏷️ Status Badges

BadgeColorMeaning
● ReturningGreenRenewed and confirmed to return. More seasons are coming even if no air date is set yet.
■ EndedGreyConcluded naturally on the creators' own terms. A deliberate ending — not cancelled.
✕ CancelledRedCancelled by the network or streamer before a natural conclusion. No more episodes.
◆ In ProductionAmberA new season is currently being filmed. Episodes aren't available yet.
◇ PlannedBlueA future season announced but not yet in production.

Ended vs Cancelled

Ended — finished on its own terms. Cancelled — axed before a natural conclusion, often leaving storylines unresolved. Marquee uses different colors (grey vs red) so you know at a glance whether the ending was intentional.

Why does "Returning" show with no air date?

"Returning Series" means the show is renewed but the next season's premiere date hasn't been announced yet. Once a date is confirmed on TMDB, Marquee detects the change and fires a "Season date announced" alert.

🎬 Episode Tracking

Season Pill Selector

Seasons appear as a horizontal row of pill buttons — [ S1 ] [ S2 ] [ S3 ]. Tap any pill to load that season's episodes below. The first unwatched season is auto-selected when you expand a show.

  • S1 ✓ (green) — fully watched
  • S2 · (amber dot) — in progress
  • S3 (plain) — not started

Hide / Show Watched Seasons

Fully-watched seasons are hidden by default. "👁 Show Watched (N)" on the right of the pill row reveals them. The number tells you how many are hidden. Toggle back with "🙈 Hide Watched."

Marking Episodes Watched

Click any episode row to toggle it watched/unwatched. Future (unaired) episodes are dimmed and not clickable.

Hide / Show Watched Episodes

Within each season, watched episodes are hidden by default. "👁 Show Watched (N)" in the season header reveals them. Toggle with "🙈 Hide Watched."

Episode Summaries

Episodes with a TMDB summary show a ▾ chevron after the title. Tap the title to expand the summary inline — the chevron rotates to ▴ and turns amber while open. No chevron means no summary is available.

Watch History

Each watched episode shows a green timestamp below the air date — "watched 2d ago" — using the exact time you checked it off, formatted in your selected timezone.

Mark All / Unmark

✓ Mark All in the season header marks every aired episode in one click. Shows ↩ Unmark when the season is fully watched. Future episodes are skipped.

Progress in List View

Each list row shows a "X/Y eps" counter and thin progress bar on the right (hidden on mobile to save space).

▶ Up Next Widget

Appears at the top of the Watching tab. For each show with unwatched aired episodes, Up Next surfaces the earliest unwatched episode — exactly where to pick up next.

Mark as Watched button

Each card has a ✓ Mark as Watched button. Clicking it marks that episode, logs it to the Feed, and automatically advances the card to the next unwatched episode — no need to open the detail panel.

Why doesn't a show appear?

  • All aired episodes are already watched (you're caught up)
  • The show has no aired episodes yet (everything is future-dated)
  • The show is in Watchlist or Completed, not Watching

📺 Streaming & Plex

Streaming Icons in List View

Provider logos (Netflix, Max, Hulu, Apple TV+, etc.) appear in each show's list row to the left of the episode progress bar. US flatrate/subscription services only — not rentals or free-with-ads. Loaded lazily after the list renders and cached for the session.

Plex Flag

Marquee can't automatically detect your Plex library — use the manual toggle instead:

  1. Open any tracked show's detail panel
  2. Click "📦 Add to Plex" below the action buttons
  3. It turns amber and shows "📦 On Plex ✓"
  4. A gold P badge appears in the show's list row
  5. The Plex flag also appears in your daily digest email for shows airing that day

Click the button again to remove the flag. Stored in Supabase per show.

📧 Daily Email Digest

Marquee sends one email per day at 6 AM Eastern summarizing everything happening with your tracked shows. You always receive an email — even on quiet days.

How It Works

  1. A background job runs hourly to detect changes on your tracked shows and write alerts to the database. No email is sent at this stage.
  2. A separate job runs daily at 6 AM Eastern to collect the past 24 hours of alerts, check which shows are airing today, and send your digest email.
  3. This means you see changes in the in-app Alerts tab within an hour, and receive the email summary the next morning.

What the Email Contains

  • Smart subject line — "Marquee: 3 episodes airing, 1 premiere, 2 show updates today"
  • Summary chips — quick at-a-glance count of airing episodes, premieres, and show updates
  • 📺 Airing Today — every tracked show with an episode airing that day, showing season/episode number, "SEASON PREMIERE" badge for new seasons, network name, and a Plex badge if you've flagged the show as in your library
  • 🔔 Show Updates — all other alerts from the past 24 hours, grouped by type
  • Quiet day — "All quiet today — no new episodes or updates. Enjoy your day!"

What Triggers a Show Update alert

AlertWhen
📅 New Air DateShow had no confirmed date, now has one
📅 Date ChangedAir date was rescheduled to a different date
🆕 New Season AddedA new season appeared on TMDB
🚫 CancelledShow status changed to Canceled
🏁 EndedShow status changed to Ended
🔄 RenewedShow confirmed returning for a new season
🎬 Now FilmingShow entered production

Timezone in Emails

Dates in your digest email are formatted in the timezone you selected in Settings. If you haven't changed it, Eastern Time is the default.

Will I get repeat alerts?

No. Each event has a unique key — the same alert can't be written to the database twice. The digest only includes alerts that were genuinely new in the past 24 hours.

❓ FAQ

How often does Marquee update show information?
Every time you open or refresh the app, live TMDB data is fetched for all tracked shows. The background job also checks hourly. Show statuses, air dates, and episode data are always current.
Why doesn't a show appear in Up Next even though I have episodes to watch?
Up Next only shows shows in your Watching list with aired unwatched episodes. If the show is in Watchlist or Completed, or if all unwatched episodes are future-dated, it won't appear.
Why are all my episodes hidden when I open a season?
Watched episodes are hidden by default. Click "👁 Show Watched (N)" in the season header to reveal them. Fully-watched seasons are also hidden — click "👁 Show Watched (N)" in the pill row to reveal those too.
A show with unwatched episodes appeared in the "Upcoming" filter instead of "Unwatched."
The Unwatched filter is strictly date-based — it only shows shows where the unwatched episodes have already aired (air date ≤ today). A show appears in Upcoming when you're caught up on all aired episodes and a future episode has a confirmed air date. If a show seems misplaced, refreshing the page rebuilds the episode progress cache which may correct it.
The episode summary doesn't expand when I tap the title.
Only episodes with a TMDB summary show a ▾ chevron. No chevron means TMDB doesn't have a summary for that episode — common for very recent or niche shows.
The ratings panel shows N/A for everything except TMDB.
Ratings come from the OMDb API. Make sure your OMDb key is activated via the email from omdbapi.com. If activated but still N/A, OMDb may not have data for that specific title.
Why doesn't "You Might Also Like" show obvious franchise connections?
Marquee fetches both TMDB's /recommendations and /similar endpoints, merges them with recommendations ranked first. This is much better than /similar alone and usually surfaces direct connections like spinoffs and same-creator shows. For anything it still misses, an AI-powered recommendations feature is planned.
No streaming icons appear for some shows.
Only US flatrate (subscription) services appear — not rentals, purchases, or free tiers. Data comes from TMDB's JustWatch integration and may lag real-world availability by a few days.
How do I mark a show as being in my Plex library?
Open the show's detail panel and click "📦 Add to Plex" below the action buttons. A gold P badge appears in the list row. Click again to remove. The flag also appears in your daily digest email for shows airing that day.
I'm not receiving my daily digest email.
The digest sends at 6 AM Eastern. Check your spam folder first. If it's not there, verify the Edge Function is deployed in Supabase and the daily cron job is active. You can test it manually in the Supabase Edge Functions dashboard by invoking the function with body {"mode":"digest"}.
Can I use Marquee on my phone?
Yes — fully responsive on mobile. Open the drawer with ☰ or by swiping right from the left edge. Add it to your home screen via "Add to Home Screen" in your browser for an app-like experience.
My magic link says "expired or invalid."
Make sure the Site URL in Supabase → Authentication → URL Configuration matches your live app address. Magic links expire after a short window — request a fresh one after fixing the URL.
Can other users see my shows?
No. Row Level Security is enforced at the PostgreSQL level — nobody can read or modify another user's data.
Will the app get slower as I add more shows?
The main consideration is initial load — one TMDB request per tracked show on every page load. Fast up to ~200 shows. A future update will cache TMDB metadata in Supabase to make loads instant at any library size.
