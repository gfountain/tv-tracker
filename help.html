🚀 Getting Started

Signing In

Marquee uses Supabase Auth. On the sign-in screen you have three options:

  • Email + Password — enter your email and password and click Sign In.
  • Sign Up — switch to the Sign Up tab, enter email and password. If email confirmation is required, check your inbox first.
  • Magic Link — enter your email and click "✨ Send Magic Link Instead." You'll receive an email with a link that signs you in instantly, no password required.

Session Persistence

Marquee stays signed in across browser restarts automatically — Supabase stores your session token in localStorage. You'll only be asked to sign in again if you explicitly sign out (the 🚪 button in the topbar) or if your session expires after a very long time.

Who Can Sign Up?

Marquee is configured for a small group of invited users — public sign-ups may be disabled. If you can't sign up, contact the person who set up Marquee and ask them to send you an invite from the Supabase dashboard.

Your Data is Private

Every piece of data in Marquee — your shows, episode progress, alerts, activity — is scoped to your account via Supabase Row Level Security. Even if two people use the same Marquee instance, neither can see the other's data. This is enforced at the database level, not just in the UI.

Removing a Show

Open the detail panel and click the red Remove button. This deletes the show, all its episode progress, and any alerts associated with it. This cannot be undone.

🏷️ Status Badges

Every show card and detail panel shows a status badge reflecting the show's current production status on TMDB. Here's what each one means:

BadgeColorMeaning
● Returning Green The show has been renewed and is confirmed to return for at least one more season. A future season exists but may not have a confirmed air date yet. This is the healthiest status — the show is alive and more is coming.
■ Ended Grey The show has concluded naturally. The creators chose to end it — it wasn't cancelled. All episodes have aired. Examples: Breaking Bad, The Wire, Schitt's Creek.
✕ Cancelled Red The show was cancelled by the network or streaming service, typically before the story reached a natural conclusion. No more episodes will be made.
◆ In Production Amber A new season is currently being filmed. An air date may or may not be confirmed yet. The show is active but episodes aren't available yet.
◇ Planned Blue A future season has been announced but production hasn't started yet. The least certain status — plans can change.

What's the difference between Ended and Cancelled?

Ended means the show finished on its own terms — the creators wrapped the story. Cancelled means the network or streaming service pulled the plug, often leaving storylines unresolved. TMDB makes this distinction and Marquee reflects it with different colors (grey vs red) so you know at a glance whether the ending was intentional.

Why does a show say "Returning" but have no air date?

"Returning Series" is TMDB's status for any show that's been renewed, even if the next season's premiere date hasn't been announced yet. The renewal is confirmed, but the schedule isn't. Once a date is set, TMDB adds it and Marquee will detect the change and generate a "Season date announced" notification.

🎬 Episode Tracking

Season Accordions

In the show detail panel (and in the inline list-view expansion), seasons are shown as collapsible accordions. Click a season header to expand it and see all episodes. Click it again to collapse. Multiple seasons can be open at the same time.

Marking Episodes Watched

Click any episode row to toggle it between watched (green ✓) and unwatched. Future episodes that haven't aired yet are shown dimmed and aren't clickable. The season stays expanded while you check off episodes — it won't collapse on you.

Season Progress

Each season header shows:

  • A watched/total count (e.g. "7/10")
  • A mini progress bar that fills amber as you watch
  • A circle checkmark — turns green when the season is fully watched

Mark All / Unmark All

Each season header has a ✓ All button that marks every aired episode in that season as watched in one click. If all episodes are already watched it shows ↩ to unmark them all. Only aired episodes are affected — future episodes are skipped.

Hide Watched Seasons

Fully-watched seasons are hidden by default so you only see what's left to watch. The button in the Seasons & Episodes header toggles between "👁 Show Watched Seasons" and "🙈 Hide Watched Seasons." The setting is per-show and per-session (not saved to the database).

Inline Expansion in List View

In list view (≡), clicking anywhere on a show row expands it inline to reveal the full season/episode accordion — the same tracking interface as the detail panel, without leaving the list. A ▾ chevron on the right indicates expand/collapse state.

To open the full slide-out detail panel instead, click specifically on the poster thumbnail or the show title text.

Progress on Show Cards

In grid view, each show card shows a thin progress bar at the bottom. In list view, a "X/Y eps" counter and progress bar appear on the right. These counts reflect real aired-episode data — a show with 80 total episodes but only 40 aired will show progress out of 40, not 80.

How is "total aired" calculated?

Marquee uses TMDB's last_episode_to_air data to estimate how many episodes have aired, rather than fetching every season's episode list (which would be slow). The detail panel's season accordions always show the exact correct count. The card-level progress bar is an estimate that's accurate for the vast majority of shows.

▶ Up Next Widget

The Up Next widget appears at the top of the Watching tab (above the show list) and tells you exactly where to pick up on each show you're watching.

What it shows

For each show in your Watching list that has unwatched aired episodes, Up Next surfaces the earliest unwatched aired episode — not just the next season, the specific episode. It shows the show's backdrop image, show name, season/episode number (e.g. S2E4), and episode title.

When it updates

Up Next refreshes automatically when you:

  • Check off an episode as watched
  • Mark a whole season watched/unwatched
  • Add a show to Watching
  • Move a show into or out of Watching

On initial app load, Up Next loads slightly after the main list (it requires fetching season data from TMDB for each show) and populates once ready without blocking the rest of the page.

Clicking a card

Clicking any Up Next card opens that show's full detail panel, where you can mark the episode watched and see what's next.

Why doesn't a show appear?

A show won't appear in Up Next if:

  • All aired episodes are already marked watched (you're caught up)
  • The show has no aired episodes yet (everything is in the future)
  • The show is in Watchlist or Completed, not Watching

Why are some shows missing from the calendar?

The calendar fetches season data for each tracked show to find episode dates. Shows with very long run histories (10+ seasons) may have some older seasons skipped as a performance optimization — only seasons that could plausibly have episodes in the visible month are fetched.

🔔 Alerts & Notifications

The Alerts tab has two distinct systems that look similar but work very differently.

The Red Dot

A small red dot pulses on the 🔔 Alerts tab button whenever you have at least one undismissed notification. It disappears once all notifications are dismissed.

Dismissible Notifications (🔔 Notifications section)

These are one-time events — something changed on a show since Marquee last checked. They're stored permanently in your Supabase alerts table and survive page refreshes. They fire once per event and stay until you dismiss them. Generated by both the app on load and the hourly background job.

IconTypeWhat triggers it
📅Season date announcedA tracked show previously had no confirmed next air date — now it does. TMDB has a specific episode date locked in.
🆕New season addedTMDB's season count for the show increased. A new season entry was added to the show's record.
🔄Status changedThe show flipped from "In Production" → "Returning Series" (confirmed renewal) or from "Planned" into production.
🚫CancelledThe show's status changed to Canceled. Fires once, at the moment the change is detected.
🏁EndedThe show's status changed to Ended. Fires once, at the moment the change is detected.
🎬Premieres todayThe next episode is Episode 1 of a new season and today is its air date.

Dismiss individual notifications with the ✕ button. Dismiss everything at once with "Dismiss all." Dismissals sync to Supabase — dismissed on your phone, gone on your desktop too.

Live Status Sections

These sections are computed fresh every time you open the Alerts tab from your tracked shows' current TMDB data. They show what's true right now — not events that happened. They disappear automatically when the condition is no longer true (e.g. a show stops being "Returning" because it gets cancelled).

SectionWhat appears
📅 Upcoming Episodes/SeasonsAny tracked show (not Completed) with a confirmed next-episode date within roughly the next 30 days. Shows a countdown: "Today!", "Tomorrow", "3d", "2w".
🚫 Cancelled or EndedAny tracked show currently in Canceled or Ended status on TMDB.
✅ Currently ReturningAny tracked show currently in "Returning Series" status — renewed and confirmed to come back.

Live sections also have ✕ dismiss buttons. Unlike the notification section, these dismissals are snoozed in your browser's localStorage (not synced to Supabase), and they auto-expire when the underlying condition changes. For example if you dismiss an upcoming episode alert, it will reappear when that show's next episode date changes.

📧 Background Email Notifications

In addition to the in-app alert system, Marquee runs an automated hourly job that checks TMDB even when no one has the app open and emails you when something changes.

How It Works

  1. Every hour, a Supabase Edge Function runs automatically on a cron schedule
  2. It fetches current TMDB data for every tracked show across all users
  3. It compares the current data against the last-stored snapshot for each show
  4. If anything changed (new air date, new season, status change, etc.), it writes a new alert to the database and sends an email immediately via Resend
  5. The snapshot is then updated to the current state, ready for next hour's comparison

What Triggers an Email

Exactly the same events as the in-app dismissible notifications: season date announced, new season added, status changed, cancelled, ended, premieres today. The email is sent the same hour the change is detected — not batched into a daily digest.

Will I get repeat emails?

No. The system uses a unique alert_key per event with an ignoreDuplicates upsert. If the same event is detected again on the next hourly run (e.g. the show is still cancelled), no new alert row is created, and no email is sent. You only get one email per event.

What if I don't want email for a specific alert?

Currently there's no per-show or per-type email preference setting. If you dismiss an alert in the app, the email for that specific event has already been sent. The email and in-app systems fire simultaneously — there's no way to get in-app alerts without emails at this time.

Email Sender

Emails come from the configured FROM address on the tvtracker.gregfountain.com domain via Resend. If emails aren't arriving, check your spam folder and ensure the sending domain is verified in Resend.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

How often does Marquee check for new show information?
Every time you open or refresh the app, it fetches live data from TMDB for all your tracked shows. The background Edge Function also checks hourly, independently of whether you have the app open.
Why does a show show "Returning" but I haven't gotten a new season notification?
"Returning Series" just means the show is renewed — it's been that status since before you started tracking it, so there was no change to detect. Notifications only fire when something changes from one state to another. If you added the show while it was already "Returning," no notification fires for that.
The episode count on my show card seems wrong.
The card-level progress uses an estimate based on TMDB metadata. Open the show's detail panel and expand the season accordions for the exact correct count per season.
Can I use Marquee on my phone?
Yes — Marquee is responsive and works in mobile browsers. The tab bar scrolls horizontally on small screens. For a more app-like experience you can add it to your home screen via your browser's "Add to Home Screen" option.
My magic link says "expired or invalid."
This usually means the Supabase Site URL is misconfigured (pointing at localhost instead of the live app URL) or the link has already been used. Make sure the Site URL in Supabase → Authentication → URL Configuration matches your live app address. Request a fresh magic link after fixing the URL.
Can other users see my shows?
No. Row Level Security is enabled on every database table. Even if two people share the same Marquee instance, neither can read or modify the other's data — this is enforced at the PostgreSQL level, not just in JavaScript.
Where is my data stored?
In a Supabase PostgreSQL database. All show metadata (titles, posters, ratings) comes from TMDB's API and is never stored locally — only your personal data (which shows you track, episode progress, alerts, activity) lives in the database.
What does TMDB stand for?
The Movie Database — a community-maintained database of TV shows and movies at themoviedb.org. It's free to use and powers Marquee's search, metadata, episode data, streaming providers, trailers, and curated charts.
Why is a streaming provider missing for a show I know is on Netflix?
Streaming availability data comes from TMDB's JustWatch integration. It reflects US availability and can sometimes lag behind real-world availability by a few days. TMDB's coverage is broad but not always 100% current.
Can I import my watch history from another app?
Not currently. This is a potential future feature — import from Trakt, IMDb, or a CSV.
The help panel search isn't finding what I'm looking for.
Try shorter search terms. The search matches any text visible in the help content — so "badge" finds the Status Badges section, "dismiss" finds the Alerts section, "hours" finds the Stats section, etc.
