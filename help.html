🚀 Getting Started

Signing In

On the sign-in screen you have three options:

  • Email + Password — enter your email and password and click Sign In.
  • Sign Up — switch to Sign Up, enter email and password. Check your inbox if email confirmation is required.
  • Magic Link — enter your email and click "✨ Send Magic Link Instead." A link arrives by email that signs you in instantly, no password needed.

Session Persistence

Marquee stays signed in across browser restarts. You'll only be signed out if you explicitly click 🚪 Sign Out or your session expires after a very long time.

Privacy

Every piece of data — shows, episode progress, alerts, activity — is scoped to your account via Supabase Row Level Security. Even users sharing the same Marquee instance cannot see each other's data. This is enforced at the database level, not just in the UI.

Adding a Show

Type in the search bar while in TV mode. Results appear as you type with poster, year, and rating. Click + Add to add directly to Watching, or click the result to open the detail panel and choose which list.

Watching Tab Sub-Views

  • All Shows — your complete Watching list regardless of progress.

Inline List Expansion

In list view, clicking anywhere on a show row (except the poster or title) expands it inline to reveal full season and episode tracking — the same as the detail panel, without leaving the list. A ▾ chevron shows the expand state.

Click the poster thumbnail or show title to open the full slide-out detail panel instead.

Show Detail Panel

Slide-out panel showing: backdrop, poster, title, genres, rating (clickable — opens Ratings panel), air status, season/episode counts, overview, seasons & episodes, trailer, streaming providers, cast, crew, and similar shows.

Action buttons let you move between lists, remove the show, toggle Plex, or open ratings.

Auto-Complete

When you mark the last aired episode of a show that is Ended or Cancelled on TMDB, Marquee automatically moves it to your Completed list, logs it in the Feed as "Completed 🎉", and shows a toast. This only triggers for definitively finished shows — not Returning Series or In Production.

🏷️ Status Badges

Every show card shows a badge reflecting its current TMDB production status:

BadgeColorMeaning
● ReturningGreenRenewed and confirmed to return for at least one more season. The show is alive and more is coming — even if no air date is set yet.
■ EndedGreyConcluded naturally on the creators' own terms. All episodes have aired. Not cancelled — a deliberate ending.
✕ CancelledRedCancelled by the network or streamer before a natural conclusion. No more episodes will be made.
◆ In ProductionAmberA new season is currently being filmed. Episodes aren't available yet.
◇ PlannedBlueA future season has been announced but production hasn't started yet.

Ended vs Cancelled

Ended means the show finished on its own terms — the creators wrapped the story. Cancelled means the network or streamer pulled the plug, often leaving storylines unresolved. Marquee uses different colors so you know at a glance whether the ending was intentional.

Why does "Returning" show with no air date?

"Returning Series" means the show is renewed — but the next season's premiere date may not be announced yet. Once a date is confirmed on TMDB, Marquee will detect the change and generate a "Season date announced" notification.

🎬 Episode Tracking

Season Pill Selector

Seasons appear as a horizontal row of pill buttons — [ S1 ] [ S2 ] [ S3 ] — instead of a vertical accordion stack. Tap any pill to load that season's episodes below.

Pill indicators show progress at a glance:

  • S1 ✓ (green) — fully watched
  • S2 · (amber dot) — partially watched / in progress
  • S3 (plain) — not started

The first unwatched season is auto-selected when you expand a show. Your last selected season is remembered per show.

Hide / Show Watched Seasons

Fully-watched seasons are hidden by default. A "👁 Show Watched (N)" link on the right side of the pill row reveals them. The number tells you how many are hidden. Click again to hide them ("🙈 Hide Watched"). Revealing watched seasons also shows them as muted green pills.

Marking Episodes Watched

Click any episode row to toggle it watched/unwatched. Future (unaired) episodes are dimmed and not clickable. A green ✓ appears on watched episodes.

Hide / Show Watched Episodes

Within each season, watched episodes are hidden by default. A "👁 Show Watched (N)" button in the season header reveals them — the number shows how many are hidden. This lets you focus on what's left to watch without scrolling past everything you've already seen. Toggle "🙈 Hide Watched" to collapse them again.

Episode Summaries

Each episode title with a TMDB summary shows a ▾ chevron after the name. Tap the title to expand the summary inline below the row. The chevron rotates to ▴ and turns amber while open. Tap again to collapse. Episodes without summaries show no chevron.

Watch History

Each watched episode shows a small green timestamp below the air date — "watched 2d ago", "watched 3h ago" — using the exact time you checked it off.

Mark All / Unmark

The season header has a ✓ Mark All button that marks every aired episode in that season as watched in one click. Shows ↩ Unmark when the season is fully watched. Only aired episodes are affected — future episodes are skipped.

Progress in List View

Each list row shows a "X/Y eps" counter and a thin progress bar on the right. Hidden on mobile to save space.

Auto-Complete on Ended/Cancelled Shows

When you mark the last aired episode of a show that is Ended or Cancelled, Marquee automatically moves it to Completed. See TV Shows → Auto-Complete for details.

▶ Up Next Widget

Appears at the top of the Watching tab. For each show in your Watching list with unwatched aired episodes, Up Next surfaces the earliest unwatched episode — exactly where to pick up next.

What each card shows

Show backdrop, show name, season/episode number (e.g. S2E4), episode title, and a ✓ Mark as Watched button.

Mark as Watched button

Clicking "✓ Mark as Watched" on an Up Next card marks that specific episode, logs it to the Feed, and automatically advances the card to the next unwatched episode. You never have to open the detail panel just to check off an episode.

When it updates

Up Next refreshes when you mark an episode watched, mark a season, add a show to Watching, or move a show into Watching. On initial load it populates slightly after the main list since it needs per-show season data from TMDB.

Why doesn't a show appear?

  • All aired episodes are already watched (you're caught up)
  • The show has no aired episodes yet (everything is future)
  • The show is in Watchlist or Completed, not Watching

📺 Streaming & Plex

Streaming Provider Icons in List View

Each show's list row shows small streaming provider logos (Netflix, Max, Hulu, Apple TV+, etc.) to the left of the episode progress bar. These reflect US streaming availability from TMDB's JustWatch data — flatrate/subscription services only, not rentals or purchases.

Icons load lazily after the list renders so they don't slow down the initial page load. Once loaded for a show, they're cached for the session.

Up to 3 logos are shown per row. If a show isn't on any US streaming service the space is simply empty.

Plex Flag

Plex is your personal media server — TMDB has no way to know what's in your library. Marquee handles this with a simple manual toggle:

  1. Open any tracked show's detail panel
  2. Click "📦 Add to Plex" below the action buttons
  3. It turns amber and shows "📦 On Plex ✓"
  4. A gold P badge appears in the show's list row alongside any streaming logos

Click the button again to remove the Plex flag. The state is stored in Supabase per show per user.

Streaming in the Detail Panel

The "📺 Where to Watch" section in every detail panel shows the full list of US streaming providers — same data as the list row icons, just with provider names displayed.

⭐ Ratings & Reviews

Opening the Ratings Panel

In any show or movie detail panel, the TMDB rating reads "⭐ 8.7/10 · Ratings ↗" as a clickable link. Tapping it slides in a dedicated ratings panel from the right.

What the Panel Shows

SourceWhat it shows
🍅 Rotten TomatoesTomatometer % — Fresh (≥75%), Rotten (<60%). Clicking opens RT search for the title.
👥 RT AudienceNot available on the free OMDb tier — shown as N/A.
📰 MetacriticScore out of 100 — green (favorable 61+), amber (mixed 40–60), red (unfavorable). No direct link.
🎬 IMDbRating out of 10 with vote count. Clicking opens the exact IMDb title page.

External Links

At the bottom of the ratings panel are "View on Rotten Tomatoes" and "View on IMDb" buttons that open the respective sites in a new tab. RT links go to a search results page (RT doesn't expose direct show URLs in any API); IMDb links go directly to the exact title page using the IMDb ID from TMDB.

Why does it show N/A?

Ratings data comes from the OMDb API (free tier). If a title isn't in OMDb's database or the IMDb ID lookup fails, RT and Metacritic will show N/A. The TMDB score is always available since it comes from Marquee's existing data. A warning message appears when OMDb data couldn't be loaded.

📧 Background Email Notifications

How It Works

  1. Every hour, a Supabase Edge Function runs automatically on a cron schedule
  2. It fetches current TMDB data for every tracked show across all users
  3. Compares against the last-stored snapshot for each show
  4. If anything changed, writes a new alert to the database and sends an email immediately via Resend
  5. Updates the snapshot ready for next hour's comparison

What Triggers an Email

The same events as the in-app dismissible notifications: season date announced, new season added, status changed, cancelled, ended, premieres today. The email fires the same hour the change is detected — not batched into a daily digest.

Will I get repeat emails?

No. Each event uses a unique alert_key with an ignoreDuplicates upsert. The same alert won't re-fire on the next hourly run even if you haven't dismissed it yet.

Cron Schedule

Runs at minute 0 of every hour (0 * * * *). To change the frequency, update the schedule in Supabase → Database → Extensions → pg_cron.

❓ FAQ

How often does Marquee update show information?
Every time you open or refresh the app, live TMDB data is fetched for all tracked shows. The background Edge Function also checks hourly. So show statuses, air dates, and episode data are always current.
Why doesn't a show appear in Up Next even though I have episodes to watch?
Up Next only shows shows in your Watching list with aired unwatched episodes. If the show is in Watchlist or Completed, or if all episodes are future-dated, it won't appear.
Why are all my episodes hidden when I open a season?
Watched episodes are hidden by default to help you focus on what's left. Click "👁 Show Watched (N)" in the season header to reveal them. Similarly, fully-watched seasons are hidden from the pill row — click "👁 Show Watched (N)" there to reveal them.
The episode summary doesn't expand when I tap the title.
Only episodes with a TMDB summary have the ▾ chevron — if there's no chevron after the episode title, TMDB doesn't have a summary for that episode. This is common for very recent or less-documented shows.
The ratings panel shows N/A for everything except TMDB.
Ratings come from the OMDb API. Make sure your OMDb API key is activated (check your email for the activation link from omdbapi.com). If the key is valid but a specific show returns N/A, OMDb may not have data for that title — this is more common for non-US or niche content.
Why doesn't "You Might Also Like" show obvious franchise connections?
Marquee now fetches both TMDB's /similar and /recommendations endpoints and merges them, with recommendations ranked first since they use viewing behavior data. This is significantly better than /similar alone. For very obvious connections (direct spinoffs, same creator), recommendations usually surfaces them. For anything it misses, a future AI-powered recommendations feature is planned.
No streaming icons appear for some of my shows.
Streaming data shows US flatrate (subscription) services only — not rentals, purchases, or free-with-ads tiers. If a show isn't on a US subscription service, the space is empty. Data comes from TMDB's JustWatch integration and may lag real-world availability by a few days.
How do I mark a show as being in my Plex library?
Open the show's detail panel, scroll to the action buttons, and click "📦 Add to Plex." It turns amber to confirm. A gold P badge will then appear in the show's list row alongside any streaming provider logos. Click the button again to remove the flag.
Can I use Marquee on my phone?
Yes — Marquee is fully responsive on mobile. Open the drawer with the ☰ button or by swiping right from the left edge. For a more app-like experience, add it to your home screen via your browser's "Add to Home Screen" option.
My magic link says "expired or invalid."
Make sure the Site URL in Supabase → Authentication → URL Configuration matches your live app address (not localhost). Magic links expire after a short window — request a fresh one after fixing the URL if needed.
Can other users see my shows?
No. Row Level Security is enforced at the PostgreSQL level — even if two people share the same Marquee instance, neither can read or modify the other's data.
Will the app get slower as I add more shows?
The main performance consideration is the initial load — one TMDB request fires per tracked show on every page load. At 100–200 shows this is still fast. At very large libraries (300+ shows) it could become noticeable. A future update will cache TMDB metadata in Supabase to make initial loads instant regardless of library size.
