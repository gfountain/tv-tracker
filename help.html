✨ What's New

The most recent additions to Marquee. See Release History for the full changelog.

  • Light / Dark / Auto theme — a 🌓 button in the topbar cycles between Auto (time-based), ☀️ Light, and 🌙 Dark. Auto switches at 7 AM and 8 PM. Preference saved to localStorage.
  • My Shows consolidation — Watching, Watchlist, and Completed tabs merged into a single My Shows tab with seven filter chips: All, Unwatched, Upcoming, Filming, Ended, Completed, and Watchlist (visually separated).
  • 🔔 Notification panel — the bell moved before the search bar in topbar row 1. Tapping it opens a dropdown notification panel (desktop) or full-width slide-down (mobile). Never leaves the current page. Replaces the dedicated Alerts tab entirely.
  • Topbar icon order — left to right: 🔔 Bell · ❓ Help · ⚙️ Settings · 🌓 Theme · Email · 🚪 Sign Out.
  • Toast improvements — toasts last 5 seconds, fade out smoothly, and show an Undo button after destructive actions like removing a show.
  • Back to top button — a ↑ button appears bottom-left after scrolling 400px down. Smooth scroll back to top.
  • Empty state illustrations — every filter view now has a descriptive message and a contextual action button (e.g. "See Upcoming →" when caught up, "Search for shows" when the library is empty).
  • 🗓 On This Day widget — appears at the top of the Feed tab. Shows activity from previous years on today's date grouped by year. "1 year ago you completed Breaking Bad."
  • 📅 Coming Up widget — also in the Feed tab. Shows all tracked shows with a next episode airing within 30 days, sorted soonest first with a countdown.
  • show_cache table — TMDB metadata cached in Supabase for instant list loading. 160 shows load in one fast query instead of 160 TMDB requests. Cache refreshes daily via the digest job.
  • Daily email digest — one email per day at 6 AM Eastern (EDT). White design, smart summary, Airing Today section, Show Updates grouped by type. Quiet-day message when nothing changed. Note: arrives at 5 AM during Eastern Standard Time (November–March).
  • Timezone setting — in ⚙️ Settings. Eastern, Central, Mountain, Pacific. Saved to Supabase, syncs across devices. Used throughout the app and in digest emails.

🚀 Getting Started

Signing In

  • Email + Password — enter your credentials and click Sign In.
  • Sign Up — switch to Sign Up, enter email and password.
  • Magic Link — enter your email and click "✨ Send Magic Link Instead." A link arrives by email — no password needed.

Session Persistence

Marquee stays signed in across browser restarts. You'll only be signed out if you click 🚪 Sign Out or your session expires.

Privacy

All data is scoped to your account via Supabase Row Level Security — enforced at the database level. Even users sharing the same Marquee instance cannot see each other's data.

📺 My Shows

Adding a Show

Type in the search bar while in TV mode. Click + Add to add to Watching, or open the detail panel to choose a list.

Filter Chips

Seven filter chips appear below the section header. Each shows a count badge:

FilterLogicBadge color
AllEvery tracked show across all statuses—
UnwatchedHas at least one aired episode you haven't watched — strictly date-based, future episodes never countBlue
UpcomingCaught up on all aired episodes + a confirmed future air dateBlue
FilmingTMDB status = In ProductionPurple
EndedTMDB status = Ended or CancelledRed
CompletedShows you've personally finished (auto-moved when last episode marked)Green
WatchlistShows you intend to watch but haven't started (dashed border, visually separated)—

Filters are not mutually exclusive — a show can appear in both Ended and Unwatched if it's cancelled but you haven't finished watching it. The default view is Unwatched.

How Shows Move Between Filters

Shows move between filters automatically based on two things: your watch progress and TMDB data. You never manually move a show between filters — Marquee handles it.

Returning shows (the most common case)

Your stateWhere it appears
Has aired episodes you haven't watchedUnwatched
Caught up + confirmed future air dateUpcoming
Caught up + no confirmed date yetAll only — not in any specific filter
New episode dropsAutomatically moves back to Unwatched

Ended / Cancelled shows

Your stateWhere it appears
Episodes left to watchUnwatched and Ended
All episodes marked watchedAuto-moves to Completed — disappears from Unwatched and Ended

In Production / Filming shows

Your stateWhere it appears
Caught up, no air date yetFilming and All
New season premieresMoves to Upcoming then Unwatched as episodes air

Key things to know

  • Unwatched is strictly date-based — only episodes with an air date on or before today count. Future episodes never make a show appear in Unwatched.
  • TMDB sometimes lags — if an episode aired today but TMDB hasn't updated yet, Marquee uses the next episode's air date to detect it. The show will appear in Unwatched within a few hours.
  • Caught up with no date — a returning show you're fully caught up on with no announced next air date sits in All only. This is correct — there's nothing to watch and nothing confirmed coming. It will move to Upcoming automatically when a date is announced.
  • Filter changes are instant — when you mark the last episode of a show watched, it disappears from Unwatched immediately without requiring a refresh.

Inline List Expansion

Click anywhere on a show row (except the poster or title) to expand it inline for season/episode tracking. Click the poster or title to open the full slide-out detail panel.

Show Detail Panel

Slide-out showing: backdrop, poster, title, genres, rating (tap → Ratings panel), seasons & episodes, trailer, streaming providers, cast, crew, similar shows, and Plex toggle.

Auto-Complete

When you mark the last aired episode of an Ended or Cancelled show, Marquee automatically moves it to Completed, logs it in the Feed with 🎉, and shows a toast. Only fires for definitively finished shows — not Returning or In Production.

Removing a Show

Open the detail panel and click Remove. A toast appears with an Undo button — tap it within 5 seconds to restore the show without losing any episode progress.

Sort & Genre Filter

Sort by Name (A–Z, default), Recently Added, Next Air Date, or Rating. Genre chips appear below the filter bar — on mobile they collapse behind a "🎭 Genre ▾" toggle. The filter bar scrolls horizontally on mobile.

Episode Summaries

Episodes with a TMDB summary show a ▾ chevron after the title. Tap the title to expand. Tap again to collapse. No chevron = no summary available.

Watch History

Each watched episode shows a green timestamp — "watched 2d ago" — formatted in your selected timezone.

Mark All / Unmark

✓ Mark All marks every aired episode in one click. Shows ↩ Unmark when fully watched. Future episodes are skipped.

▶ Up Next Widget

Appears at the top of the Unwatched and All filter views. Shows the earliest unwatched aired episode for each show in your Watching list.

The ✓ Mark as Watched button on each card marks that episode, logs it to the Feed, and auto-advances to the next unwatched episode — without opening the detail panel.

Why doesn't a show appear?

  • All aired episodes are already watched (you're caught up)
  • The show has no aired episodes yet (all future-dated)
  • The show is in Watchlist or Completed

📧 Daily Email Digest

One email per day at 6 AM Eastern (EDT) / 5 AM Eastern (EST) summarizing everything happening with your tracked shows. Always sent — even on quiet days.

How It Works

  1. Every 6 hours a background job detects changes on your tracked shows and writes alerts to the database. No email at this stage.
  2. At 6 AM Eastern daily (10 AM UTC in summer, 11 AM UTC in winter) a separate job fetches fresh TMDB data, updates the show cache, reads the past 24 hours of alerts, and sends your digest.

What the Email Contains

  • Smart subject — "Marquee: 3 episodes airing, 1 premiere, 2 show updates today"
  • Summary chips — quick count of airing episodes, premieres, and show updates
  • 📺 Airing Today — every tracked show with an episode airing that day. Shows season/episode number, "SEASON PREMIERE" badge for E1, network name, and 📦 Plex badge if flagged
  • 🔔 Show Updates — all other alerts from the past 24 hours, grouped by type
  • Quiet day — "All quiet today — no new episodes or updates. Enjoy your day!"

Alert Types in Digest

AlertWhen
📅 New Air DateShow had no confirmed date, now has one
📅 Date ChangedAir date rescheduled
🆕 New Season AddedSeason count increased on TMDB
🚫 CancelledStatus changed to Canceled

No repeat emails

Each alert has a unique key — the same event can only be stored once. The digest only includes genuinely new alerts from the past 24 hours.

❓ FAQ

How often does show data update?
On every page load, the app reads from the show cache (Supabase). The cache is refreshed daily at 6 AM Eastern by the digest job (delivered around 6 AM EDT in summer, 5 AM EST in winter). The background alert detection runs every 6 hours separately. So list view data is at most 24 hours stale; alert detection is at most 6 hours behind real-world changes.
Why doesn't a show appear in Unwatched even though I have episodes to watch?
The Unwatched filter is strictly date-based — only shows with aired episodes (air date ≤ today) that you haven't checked off. If your unwatched episodes are all future-dated, the show belongs in Upcoming instead. Refreshing the page rebuilds the episode progress cache which may correct edge cases.
Why are all my episodes hidden when I open a season?
Watched episodes and fully-watched seasons are hidden by default. Click "👁 Show Watched (N)" in both the season pill row and the season header to reveal them.
The episode summary doesn't expand when I tap the title.
Only episodes with a TMDB summary show a ▾ chevron. No chevron = no summary available for that episode.
The ratings panel shows N/A for everything except TMDB.
Make sure your OMDb API key is activated via the email from omdbapi.com. If activated but still N/A, OMDb may not have data for that specific title — more common for non-US or niche content.
I accidentally removed a show. Can I get it back?
Yes — immediately after removing a show, a toast appears with an Undo button. Tap it within 5 seconds to restore the show including all episode progress. If the toast has expired, just search for and re-add the show (episode progress is also deleted on removal, so you'd need to re-mark episodes).
Why did my digest email arrive at 7 AM instead of 6 AM?
The digest cron job runs at 10 AM UTC. During Eastern Daylight Time (EDT, March–November) that's 6 AM. During Eastern Standard Time (EST, November–March) that's 5 AM — one hour earlier. If you received it at 7 AM, the cron job may have been set to 11 AM UTC (the old schedule). Check Supabase → Database → pg_cron and confirm the marquee-digest job runs at 0 10 * * *.
Why is a show I'm caught up on not appearing in Upcoming?
Upcoming only shows shows with a confirmed future air date set in TMDB. If a show is renewed but no premiere date has been announced yet, it sits in All only — this is correct, there's nothing confirmed to show yet. It will move to Upcoming automatically once TMDB has a date.
Check your spam folder first. If not there, verify the Edge Function is deployed in Supabase and the daily cron job (marquee-digest) is active. You can test manually in Supabase → Edge Functions → notify-users → Invoke with body {"mode":"digest"}.
My theme doesn't persist between devices.
Theme preference is stored in browser localStorage (per device), not Supabase. This is intentional — different devices may have different ambient lighting. Set the theme separately on each device you use.
No streaming icons appear for some shows.
Only US flatrate (subscription) services — not rentals, purchases, or free tiers. Data comes from TMDB's JustWatch integration and may lag real-world availability by a few days.
Can I use Marquee on my phone?
Yes — fully responsive. Use ☰ or swipe right from the left edge to open the drawer. Add Marquee to your home screen via your browser's "Add to Home Screen" option for an app-like experience.
Can other users see my shows?
No. Row Level Security is enforced at the PostgreSQL level — nobody can read or modify another user's data even if they share the same Marquee instance.
The app loads slowly on first visit.
On first load after the show cache is empty, the app fetches all tracked shows from TMDB in batches of 10. You'll see shows appear progressively. After that first load the cache is populated and every subsequent load is instant. The cache also refreshes daily automatically.
